Movie Review - Hawk the Slayer

Jan. 12 2021

A long term goal of mine has always been to improve my story telling skills. I'd get what I suspected were "good ideas", but never acted on them either out of fear it wouldn't become to be what imagined it to be, or just pure laziness. As my Dad is always saying, "Everyone has a great idea", or in other words, if you have million dollar idea then why aren't you a millionaire?

What does this have to do with an 80's fantasy movie? Nothing. However; as of late, in order to stir up inspiration, I have decided to check out some of the fantasy movie genre. Admittidly, I will confess a bias towards older movies. This mainly has to do with wanting story rather than heavy action. Anyway, coming across Hawk the Slayer was a surprise to me mostly due to the production value. There are several titles that immediatly suffer due to grainy quality film.

Hawk

The movie starts out getting straight to the point. Voltan (Jack Palance) is sinister (and also wears a helmet masking half of his face), and will kill his own father to gain what he believes is rightfully his. The last elfin mindstone. Not as powerful as this one, but nevertheless gives the bearer Hawk physic abilities.

On the King's deathbed he gives Hawk (John Terry) the elfin mindstone forged into the hilt of sword. Hawk is then infused with the confidence to tell bandits to hit the road or die where they stand. And die they do.

The first scene is visually memorable. Golden painted walls (you could easily put your fist through) aside, the lighting used throughout the scenes lends to the mysticism at play.

"Think of the great sword in your hand, and it will be so."

Hawk is now infused with the sword's lifeforce. Voltan is going down.

"Voltan! You will die by the sword."

Ranulf

A war-torn, one-handed soldier is on the brink of death. He wanders onto the Sisterhood of The Holy Word, who nurse him back to health. Ranulf reminds me of Lord Davos from GoT. His character is the do "right at any cost" type that you would want as your second in command. Apparently, Voltan has been up to no good, destroying Ranulf's village in the process and is headed his way.

After failing to stop the kidnapping of one of the Sisterhood, he must seek out an answer to redeem his honor. Onward to the holy fortress to speak to the High Abbot. Again, another picturesque shot.

"You must go to the holy fortress of Danesford."

Reluctant to give into ransom, both the High Abbot and Ranulf believe the answer is to stand their ground. Obviously, the High Abbot is off his rocker and has no idea how bad of a dude Voltan is. However, his widom prevails tasking Ranulf to track down a mysterious warrior passing through the area.

Woman, The Sorceress

Meanwhile, Hawk is roaming the woods taking names and teaching bandits the definition of justice. He eventually comes across a "witch" in distress. The witch is actually a power sorceress accused of witchcraft and sentenced to death by two rogue bandits. By saving the Sorceress she tells Hawk his fortune. A one-handed man is searching for him, as well as the dangerous Voltan.

Although the Sorceress has a frequent role in the movie, it's unfortunate that her character is used in a way to simply fast forward the plot along. Basically her role is to round up the team to take down Voltan, but never really confronting Voltan herself. It's a shame cause she's on of the few female protaganists in the movie. Her abilities are more theatrics and illusion based. Regardless, she has a vital role.

Hawk eventually meets up with Ranulf after saving him. It's time for to earn his keep!

Roadside Tavern

This is probably my favorite scene. We finally see Voltan for the ruthless murder he is as he punishes two citizens for not standing as he passes by them: a Volton Death Grip for one, and a removed tongue for the other (for talking too much). Just the way that Voltan pushes his weight around through his words is entertaining enough. His sheer confidence in his strengths is apparent as well.

"But remember this, and remember it well. Voltan owns everything, the table, the chairs, the very food you eat. I own everything, including your useless life."

Meanwhile, Hawk is consumed in visions of his past. Voltan, who turns out to be Hawk's brother is pissed that Hawk has stolen his betrothed.

Also, tension is building in The Sisterhood of The Holy Word. They fear that paying the ransom is the only way (more on this later) to free their sister.

Ranulf is in agreement that Voltan cannot be trusted and vows to help Hawk. Therefore, it's off to see the Sorceress again to build a party to battle Voltan.

Gort, The Giant

We first meet Gort in a forest setting fixing a Trader's wagon. Nearby, a group of traveling soldiers rest and chat. The Trader has promised Gort payment for fixing his wagon, however later tries to get out of it. Gort isn't having it, and makes sure he doesn't leave after leaving the Trader without a working wagon once again.

The soldiers side with the Trader, triggering Gort to take them down with his larger than life sledgehammer. After disposing of the soldiers, Gort has a word with the Trader before noticing Hawk in the distance.

"Consider this the luckiest day of your life, toad."

Gort is supposed to be a giant and with the tricky camera angles they do a rather good job, however we know he's just a taller than average man.

Crow, The Elf

Next, the Elf. Sitting patiently for his arrowheads to be smithed, a couple of goons wander up and challenge him to an archery contest. The fact that he's an elf is of no concern to them as their game is a disguise from their true intensions of murder.

At first Crow ignores the strangers, but eventually humors them by accepting their challenge (coaxed by a wager of gold). On the count of three whoever hits their target first wins, however the strangers are playing a different game. In swoops Hawk, to mix things up like Clint Eastwood in For a Few Dollars More.

"Leave the odds as they are."

The challenge quickly turns into a fight to the death. Crow is victorious as his opponent doesn't even have time to draw his arrow.

Baldin, The Dwarf

One last member of the party, the Dwarf. Probably the least hero-like character, and felt added for character variety more than anything else. Plus doesn't even resemble a Dwarf (in all the works of fiction I'm aware of). I'd liken him more to a Gnome: rather lanky, constantly gorging on food and vulgar mouthed.

We first meet Baldin as he's being "purified" by a group of Priests. The Priests are rather terrible at archery as they can't seem to land an arrow in time before Baldin escapes. Hawk appears as well telling the Priests he needs Baldin for battle and likes him the way he is.

Baldin joins up with Hawk after fishing out a quick snack with his fierce whip (again, not a normal Dwarf weapon).

Sisterhood of The Holy Word

Hawk, Ranulf, Gort, Crow, and Baldin wreck havoc on Voltans forces to gain his attention.
